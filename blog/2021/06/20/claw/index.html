Hook Prosthetic

By Hemanth Veeraghanta | June 20, 2021

After the Innovation 4 Health competition, we decided to start making a new prosthetic from scratch. Although the features we added to the competition prototype are interesting and useful, we wanted to focus on making a prototype that prioritizes function, simplicity, and is adaptable to many people. We also wanted to move away from wrist actuated designed (when the prosthetic hand closes when the wrist is flexed), since these put excessive pressure on the wrists. Therefore, we decided to make a shoulder actuated prosthetic.

The following prototype was designed similar to the most popular existing traditional prototype created for function. The forearm piece of the prosthetic was created based on a 3D scan of Jerlena’s forearm. This prototype is great for picking things up and holding them. The claw mechanism on this prototype remains normally closed using a rubber band. A string or cable is attached on one side of the claw and is pulled open using a shoulder harness. Once open, the claw can be positioned and closed around the object.

We wanted to make the prototype as accessible as possible. For this reason, it uses 3D printed pins to connect parts together. These can easily be replaced with ¼ in bolts if hardware is available. For the first prototype, a backpack strap was used as the harness. This can be replaced with a professional harness at added cost.

About us

AICP Foundation serves to innovate and advocate for children requiring prosthetics. We plan to collaborate through a multi-disciplinary framework to design and develop 3D printed prosthetics specifically targeted towards improving impact and quality of life.
